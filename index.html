Wear the Pear!

1960

Richard was born and raised in Bogota, Colombia. Following in his father's footsteps, he came to the United States at the age of 16 to pursue a degree in Textile Engineering at Philadelphia College of Textiles And Science (now Philadelphia University). His goal was to return to Bogota and work at his father's factory, Industrias Atlas, a company that dyed, finished and manufactured hosiery, as well as texturized yarn.

1964

At the age of 20, Richard began his career managing the production of over 100 knitting machines and gradually became involved in every part of the business.

1984

In the fall, Richard and Marcy made the difficult decision to leave the family business and along with their 9 month old son, Alex, move to Philadelphia empty-handed. Within a few weeks, Richard found employment at Peter Green in West Chester, PA.

1984

Richard was hired not only to manage the warehouse but to set up their own in-house hosiery manufacturing. The entire project was in Richard's hands, figuratively and literally. Starting with five hosiery machines and two trimming machines there was no longer a need to import socks.

1992

After working for someone else successfully for 8 years, Richard made the decision to  leave everything behind and start over on his own. Pearsox was established in 1992.

2000s

Richard is an avid athlete. Shortly after moving to the states, he joined an open division soccer league. Through the years, he completed many marathons and triathlons. Later, he focused on biking and eventually completed the Leadville, Colorado 100 mountain bike race twice in under 12 hours.

2017

After 25 years, Pearsox continues to operate with the same core principles with which it was started. Customer service, quality, delivery and personal attention. Richard's belief, whether it be physical goals, family life or business, is to never give up and strive for the best you can be.
